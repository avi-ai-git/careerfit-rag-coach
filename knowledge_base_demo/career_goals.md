# Career Goals — Demo Candidate

## Target Role Profile

Demo Candidate is looking for roles at the intersection of AI, creative production, marketing, and education. The sweet spot is any role that requires both applied AI expertise and the ability to communicate it clearly — to audiences, clients, students, or internal teams.

**Primary cluster A — Creative and Technical:**
- Creative AI Technologist
- Forward Deployed Creative / AI Solutions Engineer (Creative)
- Creative Technologist with AI component

**Primary cluster B — AI Enablement and Education:**
- AI Enablement Lead
- AI Trainer / Workshop Facilitator
- AI Adoption Specialist
- Developer Advocate (non-engineering, community and education focus)

**Primary cluster C — AI Marketing:**
- AI Content Strategist
- Marketing Manager (with AI component)
- AI-native Marketing roles

**Secondary targets (as Turing College LLM course completes):**
- AI Deployment roles (where the gap is primarily technical depth, closeable within 4 to 6 weeks)
- Instructional Designer (AI / digital learning focus)
- Community Manager at AI-native companies

---

## What Demo Candidate Is NOT Targeting

- Pure backend engineering or MLOps roles
- Production-grade cloud infrastructure positions
- Roles requiring 3+ years of LangChain or similar framework deployment
- Traditional marketing roles with no AI component
- Pure UX or design roles without an AI or content component

These roles represent gaps Demo Candidate is honest about and redirects toward roles where AI expertise + communication + education experience is the primary value driver.

## What Demo Candidate Brings to a Role

### The core value proposition

Someone who can close the gap between what AI tools can do and what non-technical people actually use them for. Practically: design a bootcamp, build a prompt library, produce AI-generated content at a professional level, build a working LLM application, and explain all of it to a founder, a marketing team, or a skeptical stakeholder.

### Proof points that travel well

- First English GenAI bootcamp in the state of Thuringia — shows ability to build AI education from zero in an underserved context
- 120+ participants across six education formats over 14 months — shows scale and consistency, not a one-off
- AI film selected for Artefact AI Film Festival Paris 2025 — shows creative AI production at a professional standard
- #hikemussbleiben advocacy campaign contributed to securing €1.2M in public funding for the StarTH network — highest-stakes content production outcome
- AI Interview Practice App code-reviewed by external security engineer at Nord Security — shows willingness to build in public and be accountable for production quality
- 30% user engagement lift on LMS redesign — shows ability to measure and iterate on learning systems
- GIZ Innovation Fund Audience Choice Award from 100+ international teams — shows competitive validation of design/systems thinking

## What a Good Opportunity Looks Like

A role where Demo Candidate can:
1. Use generative AI tools daily in a professional context
2. Communicate about AI to non-technical stakeholders, students, or customers
3. Produce content, curriculum, or creative work with AI at the center
4. Be in or near Berlin, working with a team that takes AI seriously

The ideal employer sees AI as central to their product or mission — not a company experimenting with AI on the side.

---

## What Demo Candidate Is NOT

- Not a generalist marketer with no AI angle
- Not a UX-only designer
- Not a hardcore backend engineer
- Not a person who understands AI in theory but has not shipped anything
