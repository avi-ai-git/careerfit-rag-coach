# Case Study: CTRL+ALT+DEITY.EXE
*Independent AI Film Project*

## What It Is

A sci-fi short film written, directed, and produced entirely solo using generative AI tools from concept to final cut. Selected for the **Artefact AI Film Festival Paris 2025**.

**Logline:** "A grieving priest feeds the memory of his dead daughter into an AI god that promises to erase pain, until a forbidden cassette forces the system to remember."

---

## Why This Film

Most AI-generated content demonstrates what the tools can do. CTRL+ALT+DEITY.EXE was an attempt to make something that mattered — a story that uses AI's own themes (memory, loss, the limits of algorithmic consolation) as its dramatic engine. The film is not about AI in a surface way. It is about what happens when a person tries to use a machine to resolve grief, and what the machine cannot do.

The challenge was making something emotionally coherent, not just visually impressive. Generative AI tools give you unlimited visual possibility and zero narrative structure. The work was the structure.

---

## Production Stack

| Stage | Tool(s) |
|---|---|
| Story development | ChatGPT, Claude |
| Image generation | Midjourney, NanoBanana, DALL-E 3 |
| Motion and video synthesis | Veo, Kling, Runway ML, Higgsfield |
| Voice and audio | ElevenLabs |
| Final edit and assembly | Adobe Premiere Pro |

Each tool covered a specific gap. No single tool could handle the full pipeline — the work was in knowing which tool to use for which moment, and in assembling the output into something that felt continuous.

---

## What the Production Process Actually Looked Like

**Story first.** The script and visual concept were developed before any generation started. Without a locked story, generative tools produce an endless stream of aesthetically interesting but narratively incoherent material. The script was the filter.

**Visual development as a research process.** Midjourney was used first for character and environment exploration — hundreds of iterations to establish the visual language (analogue-meets-glitch, desaturated palette, liturgical motifs corrupted by digital noise). NanoBanana handled a specific stylistic layer that Midjourney could not replicate cleanly. DALL-E 3 was used for specific compositional needs where prompt adherence mattered more than aesthetic range.

**Motion as the hardest problem.** Translating generated stills into coherent motion is where most AI films fall apart. Runway ML handled the primary motion synthesis. Kling and Higgsfield were used for specific shots requiring different physics or camera behavior. Veo contributed early in the pipeline for longer-form motion experiments. Every motion clip required multiple generations and manual selection.

**Audio as the emotional layer.** ElevenLabs handled the priest's voice. The voice design choices — prosody, pacing, breath — were the only element that remained consistent across every cut of the film. Audio is where the emotion actually lands.

**Edit as the final argument.** Adobe Premiere Pro. The edit was where the film became a film rather than a collection of generated images. Cut decisions were the difference between something that felt like an experience and something that felt like a demo reel.

---

## Outcome

Selected for the **Artefact AI Film Festival Paris 2025** — an international festival specifically for AI-generated film and media work.

This is third-party validation that the work cleared a curatorial standard, not just a technical one. The selection committee was evaluating the film as a film, not as a technical demonstration.

---

## What This Project Demonstrates

- **End-to-end solo production:** concept, script, visual development, motion synthesis, audio, edit — one person, no team
- **Multi-tool pipeline expertise:** ability to evaluate and select tools by capability, not by brand loyalty
- **Creative direction with generative tools:** generation is easy; having something to say is the constraint
- **International festival selection:** external validation that the work meets a professional standard

**Relevant for:** Creative AI Technologist, Forward Deployed Creative, AI content production roles, any role where "can you actually make things" matters more than "do you know the theory."
