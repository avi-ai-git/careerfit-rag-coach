# Career Principles — Demo Candidate

## Core Career Thesis

The gap that matters most in AI right now is not between what AI can do and what researchers know. It is between what AI can do and what non-technical people actually use. Demo Candidate's career is built on closing that gap — as an educator, a content producer, a tool builder, and a communicator.

This is not a positioning statement. It is the organizing principle behind every role choice, every project, and every learning investment over the past several years.

---

## What Demo Candidate Optimizes For

### 1. Shipped outcomes over theoretical knowledge
The film exists. The apps are live. The bootcamp happened with real people in the room. Demo Candidate has a consistent preference for work that produces something verifiable over work that produces expertise that cannot be demonstrated. This is not anti-intellectual — it is a belief that the test of understanding is whether you can make something with it.

### 2. Bilingual effectiveness
English and German at professional level. In the German market, the ability to teach, present, facilitate, and communicate about AI in German is a meaningful differentiator. This is not a soft credential — it is the difference between reaching a German-speaking founder cohort and not reaching them.

### 3. Honest gap acknowledgment
Demo Candidate does not oversell. The AI Interview Practice App's security gaps were posted publicly. Skills assessments in this knowledge base distinguish strong from developing from limited. The underlying belief: an honest assessment of what you can and cannot do is more valuable to a potential employer than a polished but misleading one, because the misleading version fails in the first month on the job.

### 4. Creative depth as a differentiator
Most people who work in AI enablement cannot make a film. Most filmmakers cannot build an app. Demo Candidate can do both. This is not about being a generalist — it is about having creative credibility that makes AI education and content work land differently. The film is not a side project. It is proof that the creative claims are real.

### 5. Building in public
LinkedIn posts about what went wrong (security finding on the Interview App). Public YouTube recordings of bootcamp sessions. Portfolio with live URLs. The preference is for work that can be examined, not just described.

---

## What Demo Candidate Will Not Do

- Take a role that has no AI component, regardless of seniority or compensation (above floor)
- Oversell technical depth that has not been demonstrated in a shipped project
- Accept roles that require significant German relocation outside Berlin
- Claim expertise in production-grade ML infrastructure or MLOps

---

## On the Balance Between Creative and Technical Work

Demo Candidate sits at the intersection of creative work (film, content, campaigns) and technical work (apps, RAG pipelines, LLM applications). This is not a split identity. It is a single coherent position: the most valuable thing in AI right now is the person who can build something and also explain it, make it beautiful, and teach it to someone else.

The Turing College LLM course is not a pivot away from creative work. It is the thing that makes the creative and education work more credible and more technically grounded.

---

## What Makes a Role Worth Applying To

A role is worth applying to if it satisfies at least three of these five:

1. AI is central to the role, not an add-on
2. The work involves communicating about AI to non-technical people (teaching, content, advocacy, enablement)
3. The role has creative scope — content, campaigns, curriculum, film, design, or any combination
4. The employer is in or near Berlin, or has a genuine remote culture
5. The compensation meets the €55,000-€62,000 target range

A role that meets all five is a priority application. A role that meets two or fewer is worth tracking for future reference but not prioritizing now.

---

## On the Current Learning Investment

The Turing College LLM Application Development course represents a deliberate move toward deeper technical capability. The thesis: AI Education and Creative AI work is more credible and more differentiated when backed by the ability to actually build things. Sprint 1 produced a live app. Sprint 2 produced a more complex RAG agent. Each sprint is evidence, not just progress toward a certificate.

The career principle here is that learning is only meaningful when it produces something demonstrable. The apps are the point, not the course completion.
